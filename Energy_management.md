# Energy Management 

## 1. Emotional Quadrants — Self-Reflection

### Question 1: Calm Quadrant Activities
- Taking a quiet walk
- Listening to calming music
- Meditation or breathing exercises
- Journaling
- Spending time in nature
- Warm bath or lying down
- Reading something peaceful

### Question 2: When You Enter the Stress Quadrant
- Facing deadlines or difficult tasks
- Learning something new
- Overloaded routines
- Intense workouts
- Multitasking

### Question 3: Signs You Are in the Excitement Quadrant
- Feeling energized and motivated
- Looking forward to the activity
- Time passes quickly
- Feeling challenged but not overwhelmed
- Curiosity and mental engagement

## 2. Sleep Is Your Superpower

### Question 4: Brief Paraphrase
- Sleep strengthens memory.
- Sleep improves learning and creativity.
- Lack of sleep harms immunity.
- Poor sleep increases disease risk.
- Sleep clears toxins from the brain.
- Less sleep reduces emotional control.
- Sleep improves overall health and longevity.

### Question 5: Ideas to Sleep Better
- Maintain a consistent sleep schedule
- Reduce caffeine intake
- Keep your room cool and dark
- Avoid heavy meals before bed
- Exercise regularly
- Avoid long late-evening naps

## 3. Brain Changing Benefits of Exercise

### Question 6: Paraphrased Points
- Exercise improves brain function and memory.
- It increases growth of new brain cells.
- Exercise reduces stress and anxiety.
- It improves attention and executive function.
- It protects the brain from aging.
- Exercise boosts mood-enhancing chemicals.

### Question 7: Steps to Exercise More
- Start with 5–10 minute sessions
- Choose enjoyable activities
- Schedule exercise into your routine
- Work out with a friend
- Track progress
- Keep workout clothes/equipment ready
- Celebrate small wins
