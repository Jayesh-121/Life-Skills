# Learning Process
## 1. What is the Feynman Technique? Explain in 1 line.
The Feynman Technique is a method where you learn something by explaining it in simple words as if teaching someone else.
## 2. In this video, what was the most interesting story or idea for you?
The idea that struggling with a concept doesn’t mean you're bad at it, but that your brain is simply switching between focused and diffused modes of learning — and that anyone can learn difficult subjects with the right strategies.
## 3. What are active and diffused modes of thinking?
Active Mode: Focused and deliberate thinking used when solving a specific problem.
Diffused Mode: Relaxed and unfocused thinking where the brain forms connections subconsiously while partly occupied.
## 4. According to the video, what are the steps to take when approaching a new topic? Only mention the points.
- Break the skill into smaller parts.
- Learn just enough to self-correct.
- Remove distractions.
- Practice deliberately for at least 20 hours.
## 5. What are some of the actions you can take going forward to improve your learning process?
- Practice teaching concepts in my own words.
- Use large focused sessions with no distractions.
- Break big topics into smaller parts so that it's digestible.
- Regularly revisit concepts.
